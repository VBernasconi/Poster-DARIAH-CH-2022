The Pose Annotations Project for Artworks (PAPA) project

As you may have noticed from the use of openpose on a painting, the accuracy of machine learning models, when applied to artworks, is significantly lower. Indeed, most models are trained on real images, datasets of pictures gathered from the internet and which depict our modern western way of life. These representations are far from the pictorial realism of the Early Modern Time, which explains the difficulties of the machine when confronted to these new images. One of the many solutions to increase the accuracy of the results is to create a proper training dataset for artworks. However, the process is usually long and requires the help of annotators to mark each image with keypoints for the task of body pose estimation. The PAPA project aims to build such a dataset with the help of pationate people and propose an online platform where the photographic collection of the Bibliotheca Hertziana can be annotated in order to retrain the model primarily used to detect body poses.
