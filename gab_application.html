Gestures for Artwork Browsing (GAB) application

At an early stage in the project came the need to search for specific items within the collection of hands. However, traditional browsing tools usually require a word input, which implies annotations of images, such as a description and the metadata holding the title, author and year of creation, and some knowledge of the content of the collection. Through a series of discussions with the DVS group, the idea of a browsing tool through gestures is born. The benefits from this approach are multiple, from which: the implication of the user's personnal gestural knowledge, the possibility to put past gestures in a contemporary perspective, and to make discoveries through unexpected gestures.
With this approach, the understanding of what is present or not within the collection, as well as the very first perception of an artwork, is done through the detail of the hand. In an environment where the physical hand becomes the only access to the artworks, the question of embodied knowledge is raised, as well as the possibility for the art historian to "strike the pose" and gain a deeper understanding of the implications of the model in the depiction of gesutres.
